Overview

One of the things that sets LOONA apart from other groups is their complex lore and symbolism, commonly referred to as the "Loonaverse". LOONA's music videos portray a sequence of events and come together to create a larger story. The members portray characters who inhabit different dimensions in this story, with each having their own specific role and symbolism tied to their character. The depth of the Loonaverse lore and its themes of of love and empowerment have played a huge role in LOONA's success. References to the Loonaverse are everywhere in the group's activities- from their lyrics and music videos, to their onstage outfits and choreography. Fans from around the world have spent hours creating videos, wiki pages and lengthy social media threads in an attempt to unravel it all. This guide will give you the basics and hopefully help you catch references that a casual viewer might miss

Mobius Strip Theory

The mobius strip has many interesting mathematic properties in the real world, but both Blockberry and the members themselves have alluded to the mobius strip having symbolic significance in the Loonaverse. Fans began developing what is known as the "Mobius Strip Theory" when the official LOONA website published a graphic of a mobius strip with the group's logo in the center. The strip's three sides never intersect, but are still connected. Many fans believe this is a reference to the three dimensions that make up the Loonaverse: Earth (inhabited by 1/3), Eden (inhabited by yyxy), and The Cosmos (inhabited by Odd Eye Circle). These dimensions exist independently of each other, but the presence of the girls connects them, just as the sides of the mobius strip are connected.
